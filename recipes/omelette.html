Jean's Omelette

Grumbling Grumbling your stomach's Grumbling

Do you want to cook an omelette just as good as this image above?

Ingredients

For Filling:

  • 3 tbsp oil or butter
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 2 large russet potatoes, or any equivalent amount, diced (i kept the skin on for more nutrition)
  • 50g pumpkin, diced
  • 3 leaves round/sugar/sharp cabbage, diced
  • 3 leaves round/sugar/sharp cabbage, diced

For eggs (per omelette)

  • 2 tbsp oil or butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

For Sauce (for 2-3 omelettes)

  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • ½ cup stock
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • ½ tsp potato starch, dissolved in 1 tbsp water
  • Spices (Paprika and Parsley) - optional

Instructions

  1. Heat up oil or butter in an ovenproof pan, and put in the onions. Saute till it starts to brown. Meanwhile, preheat up the oven to 250 Celcius.
  2. Put in the potatoes, carrots and pumpkin, and stirfry until they are all coated with oil.
  3. When the potatoes, carrots, and pumpkin are half-cooked, put in the stock and pile the cabbage leaves on top. Cover with a lid and cook for about 3 mins, or until the stock starts to bubble.
  4. Uncover and mix. Pop into the oven and roast at 200 C for 20 mins, or until stock mostly dries up.
  5. Meanwhile, lets make our sauce. Heat up a little drizzle of oil, and stir fry the garlic until they start to brown.
  6. Add the tomato paste, stock and spices (optional) , and stir until it is mixed well.
  7. When the sauce starts to simmer, add in the honey and mix well.
  8. Turn off the heat and mix in the potato starch mixture. Put it in another bowl.
  9. Now for the eggs! (Winning recipes have to be a bit more complicated, right?) In a non-stick pan, heat up the oil/butter for the eggs.
  10. When the oil/butter is hot enough to solidify a drop of egg when you test it, immediately throw in the eggs and turn off the heat. Move spatula in a scrambling motion, but make sure that the overall shape is still circular.
  11. Take roasted vegetables and place some on one side of the pan.
  12. Top with cheese, and fold the omelette over. (Don’t forget to leave some cheese for the other omelettes you are going to make.
  13. Slide it onto a plate and pour the tomato sauce over.
  14. Repeat for all the other omelettes you are making.
  15. Enjoy!
